Executive Summary

Centrus Energy is a $3.3B market cap company that trades at 44x trailing earnings and generates deeply negative free cash flow (-$294M in 2026). Its predecessor company invested over $3 billion in the identical enrichment project at the same Piketon, Ohio site with the same Fluor EPC contractor and went bankrupt in 2014. The government's own contract structure — a fixed-price milestone $900M task order that puts 100% of construction overrun risk on Centrus, confirmed by DOE Inspector General Report OIG-25-25 — reveals execution risk and regulated margins, not the commercial monopoly pricing power the market is paying for. The corrected base case target is $54/share (SOTP expected value), representing -69% downside from current price. Even the full-buildout-success scenario yields $78/share — 55% below today's price. The enrichment paradox is the core thesis: the 44x multiple requires a monopoly in a large market, but if the HALEU market grows large enough to justify the revenue projections, it's large enough for DOE-funded competitors to enter. The CFO and General Counsel sold every share they owned to zero in May 2025. $350M in convertible notes convert at $97.50, guaranteeing 18.9% share dilution. The firm LEU backlog is $0.6B — 84% contingent and conditional relative to the $3.8B headline — and the January 1, 2028 statutory Russian supply ban creates a 12–24 month revenue gap with no bridge to domestic production.

The Contract Structure

Catalyst: UCA Resolution / Contract Definitization Q3 2026

The HALEU program has two distinct contract structures that together undermine the “commercial monopoly” narrative. Neither supports the 44x multiple. Centrus is a government-dependent contractor bearing first-of-a-kind overrun risk — not a regulated utility and not a commercial monopoly.

CONTRACT 1 — BACKWARD-LOOKING
Phase 2 UCA — No-Fee Since Nov 2024

Originally ~$90M cost-plus-incentive-fee. Grew to $152.3M (69% overrun). Fee suspended since November 2024. Legacy cleanup — but establishes the pattern.

CONTRACT 2 — FORWARD-LOOKING
$900M Fixed-Price Milestone — 100% Overrun Risk on Centrus

Awarded Jan 5, 2026. Pays ONLY when milestones are hit. If construction overruns occur — as in Phase 2 (69%) and USEC ACP ($1.7B→$2.3B) — Centrus bears the full excess cost. No government backstop.

"Phase two costs incurred subsequent to November 2024 have not been subject to a fee as this portion of the contract remains undefinitized and is subject to final resolution."
— Kevin Harrill, CFO · Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call · Feb 11, 2026
Why Fixed-Price Milestone Is NOT the Bull Case

The bull reads “fixed-price” and hears “guaranteed $900M in revenue.” The reality: fixed-price milestone contracts pay only when milestones are hit. Construction delays or overruns are borne entirely by Centrus. The $170M production option terms are TBD and at DOE’s discretion. The production margin — what Centrus earns operating the facility — remains completely undisclosed. The market is pricing 44x earnings on an assumption with no documentary support.

⚠ DOE Inspector General Report OIG-25-25

DOE bypassed FAR competitive contracting rules. Centrus "did not have adequate financial resources" at contract award. Cost share requirement reduced from 50% to 20%. OIG flagged inadequate DOE oversight of the program. These are not analyst opinions — they are government findings.

NOTE ON URENCO LEU+

Urenco’s LEU+ (5–10%) serves as feedstock that makes HALEU production more efficient — fewer SWUs required when starting from LEU+ instead of natural uranium. LEU+ is complementary to HALEU enrichment, not a direct process substitute. However, as a reactor fuel specification (for reactors designed to use 5–10% enriched fuel), LEU+ IS a substitute for HALEU as a reactor design choice — and commercial reactor developers are choosing it over HALEU dependency.
